### Technical Soundness Assessment

### Claims Inventory
1. **Conceptual:** STC-GS can losslessly and coherently compress 3D radar data into Gaussian parameters.
2. **Conceptual:** GauMamba predicts the $\Delta G$ of these Gaussians accurately over time.
3. **Empirical:** GauMamba outperforms 3D ConvLSTMs and Transformers.

### Verification Results
- **STC-GS Reconstruction:** Verified theoretically. The $O(N_{gaussians})$ memory complexity is sound.
- **Bi-directional constraints:** Verified. Using $L_{local}$ (optical flow) and $L_{global}$ (density distribution) to anchor the Gaussians during reconstruction prevents the "Brownian motion" failure mode where Gaussians swap places randomly.
- **GauMamba Memory:** Verified. Combining Mamba with GRU gating logic allows state-space models to retain longer-term historical tracking across sequence prediction steps.

### Errors and Concerns
- **Severe Concern (Evaluation):** As detailed in the Experimental Rigor assessment, the empirical claims cannot be trusted due to mismatched input resolutions.
- **Concern (Not Error):** The pseudo-3D flow is generated by projecting to 2D planes, using RAFT, and re-projecting to 3D. Since RAFT is trained on real-world RGB optical flow, its inductive biases on radar reflectivity slices might be highly suboptimal. The authors acknowledge this and use the energy constraint to mitigate it, which is an acceptable engineering choice.

### Internal Consistency Check
No major contradictions found in the technical derivation.

### Theory-Practice Gap Assessment
The reconstruction phase requires observing the future (backward pass from $T_{in}$ to $0$) to align the Gaussians. During the forecasting phase, GauMamba predicts future parameters sequentially. The pipeline holds together as long as the test-time inputs ($T_{in}$) can be reconstructed into a coherent initial state $G_0$ without needing the future frames.

### Overall Technical Soundness Verdict
**Sound with significant concerns** (regarding the empirical validation of the technical claims).
