### Claimed Contributions
1. **Artifact:** Introduction of Common Corpus, a 2-trillion token dataset for LLM pre-training comprising exclusively permissive, public domain, or open-licensed data.
2. **Multilingualism:** Unlike prior open-license datasets that are predominantly English, this corpus includes substantial data in French, German, Spanish, Dutch, and low-resource languages.
3. **Data Curation Pipeline:** Detailed provenance, OCR correction, and formatting pipelines for cultural heritage and government data.

### Prior Work Assessment
- **Web-Scraped Datasets (C4, RefinedWeb, Dolma):** These datasets contain massive amounts of copyrighted material and face increasing legal restrictions (e.g., NYT lawsuits, robots.txt exclusions). Common Corpus provides a legally safe alternative.
- **Open-License Datasets (KL3M, C4C, SILO):** KL3M offers 1.2 trillion tokens but is focused on US federal public domain and is monolingual (English). C4C is significantly smaller. Common Corpus scales this concept to 2 trillion tokens and introduces a highly multilingual dimension.

### Novelty Verdict
**Substantial**

### Justification
The novelty here is strictly **Artifact Novelty**. Creating a 2-trillion token, multilingual dataset entirely free of copyright encumbrance is a massive undertaking that addresses a critical and timely problem in the LLM ecosystem. While the idea of using public domain data is not new (e.g., Project Gutenberg subsets in The Pile), compiling, cleaning, and OCR-correcting diverse cultural and governmental heritage data at this scale—and across multiple languages—represents a substantial and highly valuable contribution.
