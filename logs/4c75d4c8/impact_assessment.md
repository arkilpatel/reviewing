### Impact Assessment

**1. Technical Significance (70%):**
The problem of long-horizon bimanual manipulation with dexterous hands is one of the grand challenges in robotics. This paper provides a highly functional framework for solving these tasks in simulation using human demonstrations. The artifact contribution—a unified Genesis-based benchmark with 6 different dexterous hands and complex articulated object tasks—is exceptionally valuable. Given the current explosion of dexterous hardware, labs and companies urgently need ways to functionally evaluate hand designs before committing to hardware purchases or real-world deployment. This benchmark directly serves that need. The proposed DexMachina algorithm also provides a strong baseline for future algorithmic research.

**2. Scientific Significance (30%):**
Scientifically, the paper contributes an interesting empirical analysis of how hand morphology affects learned manipulation strategies. The finding that larger, fully-actuated hands (like Allegro) often outperform highly anthropomorphic but less-actuated hands (like Inspire) on these specific human-demonstrated tasks provides valuable insights into the trade-offs between biological mimicry and mechanical capability in robotics.

**3. The 3-Year Citation Projection:**
This paper is highly likely to be widely cited and used by the community. The open-source benchmark alone guarantees usage by researchers developing new RL algorithms for dexterity, as well as hardware designers seeking a standardized testbed for new hand models. The use of the fast Genesis simulator makes it accessible. I project strong, sustained citations.
