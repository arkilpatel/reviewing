### Errors and Concerns
- **Minor Error (Equation 1 notation):** The paper writes the GRPO advantage as $\hat{A}_{i,t}$ for all tokens $t$. OSRL assigns a single scalar reward to the entire response, so advantage is at the response level ($\hat{A}_i$). Writing it with a $t$ subscript in Eq 1 is confusing notation, though practically it just means broadcasting the scalar to all tokens.
- **Significant Concern (Division by Zero in Eq 10):** Equation 10 divides by $B''$, the current valid training batch size. If a batch happens to yield zero valid tokens (i.e., all samples have zero advantage, which is entirely possible early in training or on easy datasets), $B'' = 0$, leading to a division by zero error. While the paper claims to filter easy problems, this does not mathematically guarantee $B'' > 0$.
- **Significant Concern (Off-Policy Mixing):** In the "One-step off-policy" pipeline, the initial samples are generated by policy $\pi_{m-1}$ and the MC branches by $\pi_m$. The value estimation and advantage calculation thus mix samples from two different policies. The paper does not explain how (or if) it corrects for this off-policy discrepancy in the MC value estimation (Eq 2), which assumes samples are from the same policy. Furthermore, for the PPO importance sampling ratio $\pi_\theta / \pi_{old}$, it is unclear what $\pi_{old}$ is for the trunk vs. the branches.

### Internal Consistency Check
The paper claims in text that AttnRL outperforms DeepScaleR-Preview-1.5B (Section 4.2). However, Table 1 shows that on AIME24, AttnRL scores 39.7 while DeepScaleR scores 40.5. On OlympiadBench, AttnRL scores 61.4 while DeepScaleR scores 61.8. While AttnRL is better on average, the claim that it universally "outperforms DeepScaleR-Preview-1.5B" is slightly exaggerated.

### Theory-Practice Gap Assessment
The blending of off-policy samples without theoretical corrections (or at least acknowledging the lack thereof) is a gap between the MDP formulation and the practical implementation.

### Overall Technical Soundness Verdict
Sound with minor issues / Significant concerns. The method works, but the off-policy math is glossed over and Eq 10 has an unhandled edge case.
