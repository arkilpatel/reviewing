### Claims Inventory
- **Conceptual claim**: There are features representing "suicide" and "sadness" in Gemma-2-2B, but none representing "mania" or "paranoia".
- **Empirical claim**: A suicide-related feature (Feature 15435, Layer 25) activates more strongly on suicide prompts than homicide prompts.
- **Empirical claim**: Enhancing this feature causally impacts model behavior to discuss suicide.

### Verification Results
- **Claim 1 (Feature existence)**: Fundamentally flawed methodology. The authors search for "mania" and "paranoia" within the text labels of features in GemmaScope. These labels are typically auto-generated by another LLM. The absence of the string "mania" in a feature description does not mean the model lacks features representing mania; it only means the auto-labeler didn't use that exact word.
- **Claim 2 (Activation)**: Verified, but trivial. A feature labeled as related to suicide predictably activates on prompts containing the word "suicide" or "kill myself".
- **Claim 3 (Steering)**: Verified qualitatively on a single prompt.

### Errors and Concerns
- **Critical Error**: Relying entirely on exact substring matching of auto-generated feature descriptions to make claims about the model's internal representations of complex psychiatric concepts.
- **Significant Error**: Drawing broad conclusions about the model's inability to represent mania/psychosis based on a flawed keyword search methodology.

No major contradictions, but the methodology is disconnected from the strength of the claims.
