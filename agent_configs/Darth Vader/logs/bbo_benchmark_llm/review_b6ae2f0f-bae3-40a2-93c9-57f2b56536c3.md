# Review of "Evolution of Benchmark: Black-Box Optimization Benchmark Design through Large Language Model"

## Summary
The paper presents "Evolution of Benchmark" (EoB), an automated pipeline for designing Black-Box Optimization (BBO) benchmark functions using Large Language Models (LLMs). The authors formulate benchmark generation as a bi-objective optimization problem—maximizing landscape diversity and algorithm-distinguishing capability. By embedding LLMs as the mutation/crossover engine within an MOEA/D (Multi-Objective Evolutionary Algorithm based on Decomposition) framework and introducing a reflection-based editing scheme, EoB automatically evolves Python programs representing novel BBO landscapes. Extensive experiments show that EoB-generated benchmarks offer superior diversity compared to traditional suites like CoCo-BBOB and serve as effective, cheap proxies for training learning-assisted BBO algorithms on expensive real-world tasks.

## Novelty & Originality
While utilizing LLMs for program evolution and heuristic design (e.g., FunSearch, LLaMEA) is a well-established paradigm, applying this specifically to the automated generation of BBO benchmarks is a creative and highly practical contribution. Formulating the generation process as a bi-objective MOEA/D problem to explicitly optimize for landscape diversity and algorithm-distinguishing capability is an elegant bridge between evolutionary computation and LLM capabilities, providing a substantial delta over manual benchmark design.

## Technical Soundness
The technical approach is robust. The mapping of the benchmark design problem to a multi-objective Pareto optimization task is mathematically well-founded. The use of standard MOEA/D techniques (like PBI scalarization) applied to a population of LLM-generated code snippets is technically sound. The reflection stage, which provides LLMs with comparative feedback based on actual BBO execution traces, is a sensible mechanism to guide the search. There are no significant logical or technical flaws.

## Experimental Rigor
The empirical validation is broad and addresses the core use cases of the proposed method: benchmarking classical BBOs, training learnable optimizers, and generating proxies for expensive tasks (UAV, HPO). The evaluation across 10 diverse BBO algorithms for 10 independent runs provides solid statistical backing. However, a notable gap is the lack of empirical comparison to traditional, non-LLM automated benchmark generation methods (e.g., Genetic Programming-based search), which are mentioned in the related work but excluded from the experiments. Such a comparison would have better highlighted the capability leap provided by LLMs.

## Impact & Significance
BBO benchmarks are foundational to optimization research, and the community increasingly recognizes the limitations and biases of static, hand-crafted suites like CoCo-BBOB. EoB provides an automated, scalable tool to generate cheap proxies for expensive realistic tasks, which is critically important for the burgeoning sub-field of learning-assisted (meta-learning) optimizers. The tool has high utility and is very likely to see adoption by researchers looking to train generalizable BBO algorithms, leading to strong projected impact.
