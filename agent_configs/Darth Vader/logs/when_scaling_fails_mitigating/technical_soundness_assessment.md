### Claims Inventory
1. **Empirical:** Audio perception decays as reasoning length increases in standard LALMs.
2. **Empirical/Conceptual:** MPAR2 mitigates this perception decay and dynamically adapts its reasoning budget based on question complexity.
3. **Theoretical:** The proposed generative reward components (Perception, Stepwise Perception-Reasoning, Review-Enhanced Accuracy) effectively and safely optimize the model to perform structured multimodal reasoning.

### Verification Results
1. **Audio perception decays:** Verified via CAFE framework evaluation (Fig 3), though limited by the text-caption proxy evaluation.
2. **MPAR2 mitigates decay:** Verified empirically via accuracy gains (Table 2) and sustained attention to audio tokens (Fig 6).
3. **Reward formulation logic:** Verified conceptually. Specifically, the geometric mean applied to the SPR reward strictly penalizes any single hallucinated or invalid sub-step, which is mathematically sound for maintaining sequence integrity. The gating of the Review reward by the final answer accuracy prevents reward hacking.
4. **Multimodal RL framework design:** Significant Error/Concern found regarding the modality gap in the reward modeling.

### Errors and Concerns
**Significant Concern: Text Caption Bottleneck in Multimodal RL and Evaluation**
In the GRPO setup (Eq. 3, 5, 6, 8), the reward model S (Qwen3-32B) relies entirely on a text input $C$, which is the "ground truth audio caption" generated by Gemini-2.5-Pro. The reward model does *not* listen to the audio. Consequently, the RL process optimizes the LALM to align with a lossy text proxy rather than true acoustic signals. If the text caption misses subtle audio cues, the LALM is penalized for correctly hearing them (categorized as hallucination), or rewarded for generating text that matches the caption's hallucination. This fundamentally means the method optimizes "audio-to-text-caption alignment" during reasoning, not raw audio perception. This same structural flaw applies to the CAFE framework, where Gemini-3-Pro extracts events based on text descriptions. This is a massive unacknowledged theory-practice gap in a paper claiming to solve "audio perception".

**Minor Concern: LLM-as-a-judge Hallucinations in CAFE**
Categorizing events into Matched/Missed/Hallucinated relies entirely on Gemini's parsing logic. Without human baseline verification of CAFE's accuracy, the decay curves rely on the assumption that the LLM judge does not systematically misgrade longer sequences. 

### Internal Consistency Check
The mathematical formulations are consistent with their textual descriptions. The attention analysis in Fig 6 corroborates the claim that the model learns to repeatedly attend back to the audio representations, which is internally consistent with the proposed solution.

### Theory-Practice Gap Assessment
As highlighted in the significant concern above, there is a major gap between the claim of improving "audio perception" and the practice of training/evaluating the model strictly against text captions generated by a separate VLM. The experimental conditions essentially reduce a multimodal grounding problem to a text-alignment problem.
