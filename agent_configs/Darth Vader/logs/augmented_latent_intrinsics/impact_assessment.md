### Impact Assessment

**1. Technical Significance (70%):**
The technical advance of this paper is moderate. The proposed Augmented Latent Intrinsics (ALI) method achieves a relatively small but measurable improvement over the baseline LumiNet on the MIIW benchmark (e.g., reducing RMSE from 0.240 to 0.231 and improving SSIM from 0.527 to 0.553). The integration of the RADIO model into a latent-intrinsic diffusion framework does yield better handling of complex materials like specular and metallic surfaces. However, the practical utility and broad adoption of this specific architecture are likely to be limited. Image-to-image relighting from a single view without explicit 3D geometry is an ill-posed and somewhat niche formulation. The broader graphics and vision community is increasingly moving towards 3D-aware generative models, novel view synthesis with explicit material estimation (e.g., Gaussian Splatting with intrinsic decomposition), or control-based diffusion techniques. A purely 2D feature-fusion approach is unlikely to become a widespread foundational tool for practitioners.

**2. Scientific Significance (30%):**
The scientific impact is marginal. The core scientific claim is the identification of a "trade-off" where strong semantic encoders (CLIP, DINO) harm relighting performance, whereas dense, pixel-aligned encoders (MAE, RADIO) improve it. However, this finding is not particularly surprising or paradigm-shifting. It is widely understood in the representation learning community that contrastive models like DINO and CLIP achieve semantic invariance precisely by destroying low-level photometric information through aggressive data augmentations (e.g., color jittering, random grayscale). Naturally, models trained to be blind to illumination will perform poorly on an illumination transfer task. Therefore, this paper mostly serves as an empirical confirmation of a known property of self-supervised learning objectives, rather than revealing a profound new connection or fundamentally shifting methodological paradigms.

**3. The 3-Year Citation Projection:**
Over the next 3 years, this paper will likely receive a modest number of citations (perhaps 20-30). It will be cited primarily by researchers specifically working on 2D image relighting or intrinsic image decomposition as a baseline or as part of a literature review on using pretrained features. It is unlikely to be cited broadly by the general representation learning or foundation model communities, as the insights regarding the limitations of contrastive models for dense physical tasks are already well-established in the field.
