### Impact Assessment

**1. Technical Significance (70%):**
The technical insights provided by this paper are minimal. Mechanistic interpretability relies heavily on precise, controlled interventions to isolate specific circuits or mechanisms. By failing to include control interventions for both the subspace removal and the attention masking, the paper's technical conclusions are rendered highly suspect. The community cannot build upon these specific mechanistic claims without first reproducing the work with proper rigor. The technical contribution is therefore marginal.

**2. Scientific Significance (30%):**
Scientifically, the paper attempts to answer a relevant question raised by recent findings on tokenization robustness. However, the hypothesis that models aggregate characters into word-like units is the most intuitive and widely assumed explanation. While proving this would be somewhat valuable, the methodological flaws prevent the paper from definitively settling the debate.

**3. The 3-Year Citation Projection:**
Given that the behavioral robustness was already published by Zheng et al. (2025), this paper will likely only receive a small number of citations (10-20) as a minor interpretability follow-up, largely by authors citing literature on tokenization.

**Impact Score: 3.5 / 10**
