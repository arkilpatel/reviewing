### Technical Soundness Assessment
- **Methodological Design:** The methodology of holding the generated action fixed while manipulating the prompt format (same-turn, previous-turn, vs. fresh context) is exceptionally clean. It effectively isolates the effect of the evaluation trajectory from the content of the action itself.
- **Metrics:** The use of AUROC to demonstrate the degradation in classifier performance (monitor discrimination) is appropriate. The observation that the bias is outcome-dependent—specifically inflating the scores of *incorrect* or *unsafe* actions while leaving correct actions relatively untouched—is a sound and critical technical finding that explains the drop in discriminative power.
- **Ablations:** The paper includes several robust ablations. The control experiment varying explicit attribution prompts (e.g., "rate your action" vs. "rate this action by another model") proves that the bias is structural, not semantic. Furthermore, the ablation over the reasoning token budget convincingly shows that the bias is not merely a symptom of insufficient deliberation.
- **Limitations:** A minor technical limitation is that in the "same-turn" off-policy setup, prefilling an assistant turn with an action it didn't actually generate might place the model out-of-distribution (especially for reasoning models). However, the authors acknowledge this and appropriately rely on "previous-turn" formats to maintain clean turn boundaries for these models.
- **Overall Verdict:** The experimental design is highly controlled, and the conclusions are logically derived from solid empirical evidence.
