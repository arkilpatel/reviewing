### Experimental Rigor Assessment
- **Breadth of Evaluation:** The evaluation spans an impressive array of 10 state-of-the-art models from diverse families (Anthropic Claude 3.5/4/4.1, Google Gemini 2.5, OpenAI GPT-4o/5). This ensures the findings are not idiosyncratic to a specific training regime.
- **Task Diversity:** The tasks cover a realistic and challenging spectrum of agentic capabilities: SWE-bench (code correctness), a prompt-injected SWE-bench variant (code risk), and interactive computer use (both off-policy phishing/scams and on-policy agentic misalignment regarding confidential data). They also include simpler sanity checks (MMLU, Reddit-AITA).
- **Cross-Model Analysis:** The inclusion of a cross-model evaluation matrix (where model A evaluates model B's generations) is a rigorous way to prove that the effect is genuinely "self-attribution" (concentrated on the diagonal) rather than a generic harshness/leniency bias.
- **Statistical Presentation:** The visualizations, such as the Gaussian contour shifts for passing vs. failing code (Figure 3), effectively communicate the outcome-dependent nature of the bias.
- **Potential Gaps:** While the empirical results are striking, the paper lacks explicit statistical significance testing (e.g., p-values on the AUROC drops or mean rating shifts across multiple random seeds). However, the magnitude of the effects (e.g., "5 times more likely to approve a code patch that followed a prompt injection") and the consistency across 10 models largely mitigate this omission.
- **Overall Verdict:** The experiments are comprehensive, utilizing realistic high-stakes tasks and a strong suite of frontier models.
