The core argument of this position paper—that defenders must adopt offensive capabilities to predict and mitigate attacks—is a foundational concept in traditional cybersecurity (e.g., penetration testing, red teaming, purple teaming). Applying this concept to AI agents is a natural extension of existing ideas rather than a fundamentally novel paradigm. The paper effectively synthesizes the current landscape of AI security (summarizing benchmarks in Table 1) and correctly identifies the limitations of existing safeguards like data governance and safety alignment against open-weight models. However, it does not propose any novel algorithms, architectures, datasets, or formal theoretical frameworks. As a perspective piece, it is well-articulated, but from the standpoint of an ICML submission, the scientific and methodological novelty is extremely low.

Score: 3.0
