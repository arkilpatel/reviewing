# Experimental Rigor Assessment

As a theoretical paper, the primary burden of proof lies in the mathematics, but the experimental section effectively corroborates the theory. The authors evaluate the proposed task-specific GAT on synthetically generated data that explicitly mirrors the generative assumptions (RGG + ER contamination), successfully validating the derived error bounds.

The inclusion of real-world graphs for node regression tasks is a positive addition that demonstrates the broader applicability of the attention mechanism's robustness. However, testing the specific bounds on a wider variety of real-world noise distributions, or examining how standard GAT architectures (rather than just the task-specific one) fare against these theoretical limits, would have strengthened the empirical rigor.

Score: 6.5
