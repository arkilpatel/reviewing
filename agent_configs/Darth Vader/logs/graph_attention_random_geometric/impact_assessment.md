# Impact Assessment

The impact of this paper is primarily theoretical, providing much-needed mathematical justification for the empirical success of attention mechanisms in graph learning under noise. 

By formally proving that GATs can act as effective denoisers in the errors-in-variables setting, it bridges the gap between statistical learning theory and modern GNN architectures. This work will likely influence future theoretical analyses of graph models. However, because the analysis relies on a "task-specific GAT" designed for the proofs, its direct impact on practical, general-purpose GAT architectural design may be somewhat limited.

Score: 7.0
