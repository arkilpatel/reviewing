# Review of "According to Me: Long-Term Personalized Referential Memory QA"

## Summary
This paper introduces **ATM-Bench**, a comprehensive and highly realistic benchmark for evaluating personalized AI agents on long-term, multimodal, and multi-source personal memory. Unlike existing benchmarks that focus primarily on dialogue history, ATM-Bench incorporates four years of human lifelogging data—including emails, images, and videos—with explicit temporal and spatial grounding. The authors propose **Schema-Guided Memory (SGM)**, a structured representation that unifies heterogeneous memory sources, and demonstrate that it significantly outperforms standard descriptive memory. Through extensive benchmarking of state-of-the-art memory systems, the paper reveals a substantial performance gap, particularly on complex queries requiring multi-evidence composition and temporal reasoning over years.

## Strengths

### Novelty
- **First-of-its-Kind Benchmark:** Moves beyond the "memory-as-dialogue-log" paradigm to a much more realistic "memory-as-multi-source-archive" setting.
- **Referential Reasoning focus:** Addresses the "personalized referential" problem where agents must resolve implicit cues (e.g., "my pet") from non-dialogue sources.
- **Temporal Memory Updates:** Systematically evaluates the model's ability to handle evolving or conflicting information over time (e.g., booking vs. final invoice).

### Technical Soundness
- **Rigorous Evaluation Framework:** The three-stage formulation (Ingestion, Retrieval, Generation) and the Answer-Type–Aware Metric (QS) are sound and well-justified.
- **Unified Methodology:** SGM provides a principled and effective way to normalize multimodal data for AI reasoning.
- **Solid Baseline Analysis:** The inclusion of 5 SOTA memory systems and detailed oracle bounds provides a robust technical baseline for the task.

### Experimental Rigor
- **High-Quality Human Data:** Leveraging four years of actual human lifelogging data is a major undertaking that results in a far more rigorous benchmark than synthetic alternatives.
- **Exhaustive Human Annotation:** 1,000+ cross-validated QAE pairs with human-annotated evidence sets ensure high data quality.
- **Thorough Ablations:** Detailed studies on retrieval depth, embedding models, and judge sensitivity (Appendix) demonstrate exceptional experimental depth.

### Impact
- **Defining a New Frontier:** The benchmark identifies a highly practical and common real-world problem—searching and reasoning over personal digital footprints—and provides a rigorous framework for solving it.
- **Ethical and Privacy Template:** The detailed anonymization and safety protocols provide a template for ethical personal data research.

## Weaknesses
- **Single-Subject Bias:** While the data is extensive (4 years, multi-continental), it is based on a limited number of human lifeloggers, which may introduce specific behavioral patterns.
- **High Resource Barrier:** Evaluating on 4 years of multimodal data is computationally expensive, which may limit the benchmark's accessibility for smaller research groups.

## Final Recommendation
This is a landmark paper for the field of Personalized AI. It provides the first truly realistic and challenging testbed for long-term multimodal memory agents. The methodology is sound, the data is of exceptional quality, and the findings expose critical gaps in current frontier models. I strongly recommend **Accept**.
