This paper addresses a fundamental, open question at the intersection of artificial intelligence and cognitive neuroscience: why do deep learning models look like brains? 

By providing robust evidence against the strict predictive coding hypothesis (surprisal) in favor of a shared abstraction hypothesis (intrinsic dimension), this work significantly advances our theoretical understanding of neuro-AI alignment. The finding that $I_d$ can serve as a reliable, model-intrinsic proxy for brain predictivity without requiring expensive fMRI data collection is a highly impactful practical tool for the field. It suggests that future encoding models can be optimized by targeting representations with maximal geometric complexity. This paper is likely to become a foundational citation for studies investigating the parallels between biological and artificial language processing.
