    618 B.1. Experimental Observations
    619
    620 Disclaimer: We emphasize that while the routing dynamics presented here validate the functional efficacy of the MoVE
    621 mechanismindistributing information across global memory slots, the specific linguistic interpretations are preliminary.
    622 The analysis is conducted on the D32-MoVE-×1 architecture, which is significantly smaller in parameter count than
    623 commercial foundation models and was trained on a subset of the FineWeb-Edu dataset. Consequently, while the distinct
    624 routing patterns confirm that the mechanism successfully differentiates contexts, the depth and nuance of the semantic
    625 associations observed are specific to this model’s scale and training distribution. Caution should be exercised when
    626 generalizing these specific linguistic mappings to larger, state-of-the-art systems.
    627 Thefigures below visualize the routing patterns of D32-MoVE-×1. The bottom rows of each block display the normalized
    628 absolute difference between the gating vectors. Our analysis reveals the following behaviors:
    629
    630  B.1.1. GROUP 1: STANDARD POLYSEMY (NOUN VS. NOUN)
    631
    632 In this group (Figures 3±6), the target word refers to distinct entities.
    633
    634   • Observation: In Short contexts (< 10 words), the routing is often ambiguous, with control and semantic differences
    635    showing similar magnitudes (e.g., Bat in Figure 5).
    636   • Context Sensitivity: As context increases to Long (Full Paragraph), a clear signal emerges. The Control difference
    637    drops (indicating stability), while the Semantic difference remains high. For Bank (Figure 3), the Long context reveals
    638    distinct ªvertical bandº in the difference heatmap, suggesting that specific memory slots specialize in distinguishing
    639    these concepts once sufficient context is available.
    640
    641  B.1.2. GROUP 2: SYNTACTIC SHIFT (NOUN VS. VERB)
    642
    643 In this group (Figures 7±8), the word changes grammatical role.
    644   • Observation: Unlike polysemy, syntactic shifts trigger immediate and strong divergence in this model. For Watch
    645    (Figure 7), the Short context alone yields a semantic difference (0.1617) nearly double the control difference (0.0739).
    646
    647   • Implication: ThissuggeststhattheMoVErouterishighlysensitivetosyntaxandPart-of-Speechinformation,encoding
    648    it rapidly in the global memory slots even without long-range context.
    649
    650  B.1.3. GROUP 3: METAPHORICAL SHIFT (LITERAL VS. ABSTRACT)
    651 In this group (Figures 9±10), the word shifts from physical sensation to abstract concept.
    652
    653   • Observation: This category exhibits the most subtle gradients. For Cold (Figure 10), the semantic difference is only
    654    marginally higher than the control difference across all context lengths.
    655
    656   • Implication: This ªoverlapº supports the linguistic theory of conceptual metaphor, suggesting the model may not treat
    657    abstract usages as entirely separate meanings, but rather as extensions grounded in the same semantic slots as their
    658    literal roots.
