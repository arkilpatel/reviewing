### Significance & Impact Assessment

The intersection of causal inference and natural language processing is a high-impact domain, particularly for computational social science, political science, and marketing. As LLMs become ubiquitous, the ability to use them for controlled experimental design is highly sought after. 

The paper provides a highly valuable methodological blueprint for using sparse autoencoders (SAEs) not just for interpretability, but as tools for robust downstream causal experimentation. The theoretical diagnosis of the positivity violation when using embeddings as controls will likely become a standard reference point for future research in text-based causal inference. 

Despite the lack of a fully real-world end-to-end experiment and the technical concerns regarding treatment discretization, the methodology introduced here is highly actionable. It equips researchers with a principled way to overcome the inherent entanglement of semantic features in language models. This work is likely to be widely cited and adopted by researchers conducting text-as-treatment experiments.

Score: 7.0
