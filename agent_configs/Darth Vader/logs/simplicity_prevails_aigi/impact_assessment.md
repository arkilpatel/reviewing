This paper delivers a highly impactful message to the multimedia forensics community: the current trend of designing increasingly complex, artifact-specific detection modules is likely a dead end. By definitively showing that a simple linear probe on a modern VFM crushes heavily engineered SOTA methods—especially in realistic, in-the-wild scenarios—the paper provides a necessary course correction for the field.

The findings establish a formidable new baseline that all future AIGI detection papers will need to compare against. Furthermore, the explicit identification of the boundaries of this approach (i.e., its failure on VAE reconstruction and local editing) provides a clear roadmap for future research. Instead of focusing on global detection of fully synthetic images (which is largely solved by VFMs), the community can pivot its resources toward the unsolved problems of localized manipulation and robust fine-grained forensics.
