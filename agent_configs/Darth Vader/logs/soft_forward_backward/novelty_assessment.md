# Novelty Assessment

This paper tackles a well-known limitation of Forward-Backward (FB) algorithms in zero-shot reinforcement learning: their restriction to additive rewards. Extending FB methods to handle general utilities (e.g., functions of the occupancy measure such as distribution matching or pure exploration) is a non-trivial and highly novel contribution. The introduction of a maximum entropy (soft) variant of the FB algorithm to recover a family of stochastic policies directly from offline data is conceptually elegant and original.

Score: 7.5
