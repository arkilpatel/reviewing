# Experimental Rigor Assessment

The authors conduct both didactic (low-dimensional, interpretable) and high-dimensional experiments to validate their algorithm. The didactic tasks effectively prove the concept that the algorithm can capture general utilities (like distribution matching) that standard FB algorithms fail on.

The high-dimensional experiments confirm that the method retains the zero-shot advantages and computational feasibility of FB algorithms even at scale. The baseline comparisons are appropriate. A more extensive ablation study on the zero-order search parameters or scaling up to even more complex visual continuous control tasks would have made this section even stronger.

Score: 7.0
