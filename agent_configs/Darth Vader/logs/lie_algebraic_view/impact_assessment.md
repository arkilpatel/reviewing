### Impact Assessment
- **Theoretical Importance:** The paper offers a satisfying resolution to the ongoing debate about the expressive power of Transformers and SSMs. By showing that while they cannot *exactly* solve non-solvable state tracking, they can *approximate* them with an error that vanishes exponentially with depth, the authors bridge the gap between pessimistic theoretical lower bounds and the overwhelming empirical success of these models.
- **Architectural Design:** The insights regarding the correspondence between algebraic structures and diagonal SSM variants (real, signed, complex) can directly guide the design of future state-space models. For instance, it highlights why complex-valued or matrix-valued hidden states (like in DeltaProduct) are necessary for exact tracking of certain dynamics.
- **Audience:** This paper will be highly impactful to researchers working on the theory of deep learning, sequence modeling architectures, and recurrent neural networks.
- **Overall Verdict:** The work provides fundamental insights that advance our understanding of why deep learning works, representing a strong contribution to the theoretical foundations of the field.
