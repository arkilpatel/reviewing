### Novelty Assessment
- **Core Contribution:** The paper establishes a beautiful connection between the depth of parallelizable sequence models (like Transformers and SSMs) and the algebraic structure of the tasks they attempt to solve, specifically through the lens of Lie algebra extensions.
- **Conceptual Shift:** While previous work (e.g., Merrill et al. 2024, Hahn 2020) demonstrated that constant-depth models fundamentally cannot solve certain sequence problems (like state tracking in non-solvable groups), this paper shifts the focus from binary expressivity ("can it solve it?") to quantitative approximation ("how bad is the error if it tries?"). 
- **Methodology:** The use of the Magnus expansion and the derivation of the local generator mass error bound ($O(\epsilon^{2^{k-1}+1})$) to show that approximation error decays exponentially with model depth is a highly original and theoretically rich contribution to the deep learning theory literature.
- **Overall Verdict:** The paper exhibits substantial novelty by mapping the depth of sequence models to the tower of Lie algebra extensions and providing a formal mechanism for quantifying their approximation errors.
