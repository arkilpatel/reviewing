### Claims-to-Experiments Mapping
1. Claim: VLM-RB outperforms standard prioritization methods. -> Supported by comparisons with UER, PER, ERO, ReLo, and AER on MiniGrid and OGBench.
2. Claim: VLM scores align with semantic progress. -> Supported by the "Modified Game" (Sprite Swap/Texture) ablations.
3. Claim: Mixing uniform and prioritized data is crucial. -> Supported by the $\lambda_{max}$ ablation.

### Baseline Assessment
The baselines are fundamentally insufficient for the chosen domains. 
- The tasks evaluated (DoorKey and OGBench Scene) are strict sparse-reward, long-horizon, goal-oriented tasks. The community standard for such environments is **Hindsight Experience Replay (HER)** or intrinsic motivation methods (e.g., RND). Comparing against UER and basic PER is a weak strawman, as standard TD-error prioritization is known to fail in purely sparse-reward environments.
- **Missing Critical Baseline:** The most natural alternative to VLM-RB is using the VLM as a dense reward shaping function, coupled with standard UER or PER. Without this baseline, it is impossible to determine whether the *replay prioritization* mechanism is actually beneficial, or if the agent just needed the VLM's semantic knowledge injected anywhere in the pipeline.

### Dataset Assessment
Evaluating a 1-Billion parameter Vision-Language Model on **MiniGrid** (a symbolic 8x8 or 16x16 2D grid world) is experimentally unnatural and scientifically uninformative. MiniGrid's state space is trivial; progress can be perfectly identified by a 5-line Python script. While it serves as a sanity check, relying on it for half the paper's experiments heavily undermines the rigor. OGBench Scene is more realistic, but evaluating on only three specific tasks is quite narrow for a general RL algorithm.

### Metric Assessment
Success rate and sample efficiency are appropriate metrics. However, given the massive computational overhead of querying a VLM, the paper should extensively evaluate "Wall-Clock Time to Success", rather than just "Steps to Success". A method that takes fewer environment steps but twice as much real-world time due to VLM inference is not practically superior.

### Statistical Rigor
The experiments use 5 random seeds and report standard error. While 5 seeds is the bare minimum, it is borderline acceptable. However, the performance improvements in some continuous control tasks look highly variable.

### Ablation Assessment
The ablations on the VLM size and the mixing schedule are reasonably well executed. The "Modified Game" ablation successfully isolates the VLM's reliance on visual semantics. 

### Missing Experiments
1. Comparison against a "VLM-as-Reward" baseline.
2. Comparison against HER in these sparse-reward, goal-oriented tasks.
3. Ablation on the clip length ($L=32$). How sensitive is the method to the temporal window size?

### Error Analysis Assessment
The paper does not provide a deep analysis of *why* the method fails when it does, nor does it systematically categorize the false positives/negatives generated by the 1B VLM.

### Overall Experimental Rigor Verdict
Significant gaps
