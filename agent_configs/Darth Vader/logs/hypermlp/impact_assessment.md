### Impact Assessment
**1. Technical Significance (70%):**
The technical utility of HyperMLP/HyperGLU lies in its improved expressivity over standard Softmax Attention and various linear attention baselines, as demonstrated by superior downstream performance on language modeling tasks at the 1.3B parameter scale. However, its feasibility and adoption potential are severely limited by its computational profile. The method introduces an additional $O(T^2 r_s)$ overhead per head and fundamentally modifies the attention operator, rendering it incompatible with highly optimized hardware kernels like FlashAttention. In an era where large language model pre-training and inference are heavily bottlenecked by memory bandwidth and hardware utilization, a novel quadratic-time sequence mixer that lacks a fused kernel will almost certainly not be adopted by practitioners. The authors explicitly acknowledge that achieving FlashAttention-level performance requires extensive hardware-aware kernel design, which they leave to future work. Furthermore, the field is increasingly trending toward sub-quadratic or linear-time models (e.g., SSMs, linear attention) to handle long contexts. Proposing a heavier quadratic alternative goes against this prevailing efficiency trend. Consequently, while the matched-parameter performance gains are notable, the practical, real-world impact will remain exceptionally low until these systems-level deployment barriers are resolved.

**2. Scientific Significance (30%):**
The paper's strongest contribution is its conceptual reframing of autoregressive attention as a dynamic, two-layer MLP where the context-instantiated scores form an ever-growing hidden representation. This perspective is elegant and unifies a variety of existing architectural tweaks (such as QK vs. VO compression, LoRA placement, and the removal of the probability simplex via ReLU/GLU gating) under a single, coherent theoretical framework. By mapping the behavior of attention to multi-stage memory pooling and input-conditioned sub-network selection, the authors provide a principled explanation for empirical folklore that previously required exhaustive ablation studies to uncover. This shift in understanding is scientifically valuable and may influence how researchers mathematically analyze and design future sequence routing mechanisms, even if the specific HyperMLP instantiation is not widely adopted. 

**3. The 3-Year Citation Projection:**
Over the next three years, this paper is expected to accrue a moderate number of citations (roughly 30-80). It will primarily be cited by researchers working on attention alternatives, architectural expressivity, and theoretical understandings of transformer layers. Citations will likely reference the "attention as dynamic MLP" theoretical framework or use HyperGLU as an expressivity baseline when proposing new sequence mixing operators. However, because it lacks the system-level efficiency required for frontier-scale deployment, it will not be adopted by major industrial labs for foundational model training. As such, it will avoid the massive, exponential citation growth associated with widely adopted, drop-in architectural replacements.
