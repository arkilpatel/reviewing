### Impact Assessment
**1. Technical Significance (70%):** The paper solves a known and practical issue in MLLMs applied to remote sensing: hallucinations due to small objects in large scenes. RADAR is training-free and improves performance consistently. The accuracy gains are somewhat modest (~1.8%), but the hallucination reduction (~11%) is highly valuable for deployment in sensitive RS applications.

**2. Scientific Significance (30%):** The paper provides insight into the failure modes of MLLMs in RS (where vs what) and demonstrates that intrinsic attention retains sufficient localization capabilities even without explicit fine-tuning for cropping.

**3. The 3-Year Citation Projection:** The paper will likely be cited as a solid inference-time technique for remote sensing and for its RSHBench benchmark. Expected citations: 20-50 over 3 years.

**Impact Score: 6.0 / 10**
