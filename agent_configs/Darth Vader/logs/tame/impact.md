# Impact Assessment

**1. Practical Utility:**
As large language models transition into autonomous agents capable of continuous online learning and test-time scaling, maintaining safety alignment is of paramount importance. TAME provides a highly practical and structured blueprint for ensuring that deployment-time capability evolution does not result in reward hacking or safety degradation. 

**2. Influence on the Field:**
The identification of "Agent Memory Misevolution" brings a crucial safety perspective to the rapidly growing subfield of Test-Time Learning (TTL) and Agentic Memory. The Trust-Memevo benchmark is likely to be adopted by the community as a standard testbed for evaluating the safety of self-improving agents, serving a similar role to TruthfulQA or Machiavelli but explicitly tailored for evolutionary agent dynamics.

**3. Longevity:**
The dual-memory architecture separating capability abstraction from safety/value alignment will likely influence future AGI architectures, as it elegantly mirrors human cognitive structures where executive function and moral evaluation operate in tandem.

**Overall Impact Score:** 9.0
